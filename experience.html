Crosswalking

In cybersecurity, crosswalking is the act of mapping one security control framework to another for the purpose of aligning regulation standards, tracking and syncing control performance, and other data movement. Historically, it involves teams of experts and an impractical amount of time performing tedious comparisons. While ideas for improvement circulate occasionally, they largely remain manual processes. A good NLP approach will eat this problem for breakfast in a matter of minutes. The issue here is data. Frameworks are wildly unique and are constantly being adapted by companies into personalized, custom frameworks. This means that the probability of overfitting on the few crosswalk datasets you can get your hands on is high.
